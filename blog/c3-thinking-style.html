Gregorc Thinking & VARK Learning Styles

11 January 2015

Before beginning Phase 0 of DevBootcamp, I was instructed to take a few personality assessments to determine my thinking and learning styles. According to the Gregorc Thinking Style, I am an Abstract Sequential thinker, and the VARK Learning Style claims I am a multimodal learner. (You can also take GregorcandVARK tests!) This all can sound like a ton of psychobabble, so I'll break this down further.

The following is a snapshot of my thinking style according to the Gregorc method:



I learn best by a combination of learning methods. My optimal learning process is as follows: explore new information or material on my own first, then process the information in greater detail in a lecture format, and finally work through problems or apply the material to a tangible product. I am not at all afraid of asking questions, and I often find that it helps me solidify complicated subject matter.

According to psychologist Carol Dweck, a fixed mindset assumes that an individual's intelligence is established at a young age and is static from that point onward. This ultimately leads to a constant desire to prove oneself and the simultaneous fear of failure. On the contrary, a growth mindset is a state of mind in which one believes that intelligence can be developed. This line of thinking allows for continual achievement by enabling an individual to embrace challenges and learn from failures.
